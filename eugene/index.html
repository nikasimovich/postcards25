Evening with Stephen Shore

By: Eugene Lee

Txt Img Multi

“This doesn’t look like a framed image. It is literally a real world right in front of my eyes.”

This was my first impression of seeing, my very first photobook ‘Uncommon Places’. Stephen Shore’s photobook struck me differently. I felt something that I could never feel from the digital screen.

‘Early Work’ Book Launching at Strand

Strand Bookstore has stood at the corner of 12th st Broadway, for nearly a century. Its signature red color glows even at the evening. I walked up the stairs, passing the 1st and 2nd floor. When I reach the 3rd floor, the dimness and the smell of the old books invited me.

I looked around. I see cameras. Signed books. Anticipation. Concentration.

I can hear. His words. Sentences. Past. Memories. Future.

I feel wisdom. Honesty. Time. Questions and his answers are floating around the room.

“‘Would you come and talk to the students?’ ‘Yeah, sure.’ I said.”
“‘But I have to warn you. I dropped out!’”

After the event, I held Early Work in my hands. On its first page, his signature felt like a message:

“You’ve finally met the photographer you’ve long wanted to see.”
