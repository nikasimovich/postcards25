What Are They Reading?

A public performance

By: Aliyah Mansur

Txt Img Multi

When 300 people gather to read alongside one another in the middle of New York City, it’s fair to want to snoop over shoulders, through fingers covering covers, to attempt to catch a glimpse of some bits of information that will tell you what captured each reader’s attention so thoroughly. Especially in the age of “performative reading” gendered or otherwise, it makes you wonder why a person might choose any particular book to read in front of others. Is it the same book they would pick up to read at home in privacy?

“The sky was shining blue over and over itself, like a mirror spinning on a string.”

If we are selecting our “public books” in a manner more constrained, then what are we hoping to present? Or represent? Is there something we hope to achieve? What community are we searching for through this performance of reading? And, if we truly did just pick up the book we were most interested in that day, is it possible we could be misrepresenting ourselves?
