Shattered Glass

An Evening at The Spaniard

By: Rocky Moore

Txt Img Multi

It’s a weekday at The Spaniard in the West Village. What day it is doesn’t really matter.

The bar is dark, but there’s faint lighting emulating from the candles on tables. They illuminate the glasses filled with overpriced wines and cocktails. There’s an Aperol Spritz at a table that is glowing neon.

It’s loud. Not just from the conversations of the patrons, but from the clinging of glasses. There’s the sound of ice in the cocktail shaker. The splash of a pour. Faint music, slight smacking from someone chewing, and also the traffic from outside.

The patrons are the ‘typical’ West Village crowd. Mid-twenties to early thirties. The sounds of the bar are overwhelming and you can’t really hear your own conversation, much less anyone else’s.

It gets louder and louder as it gets later in the evening. It’s so loud. The clanging of glasses increases. The conversations get louder. The music somehow gets louder. Louder, louder, louder. And then finally, a glass shatters. The bar becomes silent for a split second. The noise then resumes.

The glass shatters, the bar becomes silent, the noise resumes.
